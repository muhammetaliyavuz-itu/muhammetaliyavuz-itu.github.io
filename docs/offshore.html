Offshore Platform
Design & Multi-Tool Analysis

Rhinoceros 3D · Maxsurf · ANSYS Fluent · Complete pipeline

Project Overview

A semi-submersible offshore platform was conceptually designed and analyzed using an integrated multi-tool pipeline: 3D solid modeling in Rhinoceros, resistance and stability analysis in Maxsurf, and flow visualization in ANSYS Fluent.

Analysis Workflow

01

3D Modeling — Rhinoceros

Platform modeled as solid structure: hulls, columns, superstructure, and 25 m derrick.

02

Resistance & Stability — Maxsurf

KR Bange method up to 10 knots. Maximum resistance 1019.7 kN. Stability analysis for safe operating conditions.

03

CFD Analysis — ANSYS Fluent

Flow structure simulated at 3 m/s inflow. Pressure contours used for mooring point selection and structural load assessment.

Key Findings

  • Maximum towing resistance of 1019.7 kN at 10 knots determines propulsion requirements.
  • Pressure distribution from ANSYS identifies critical hull zones for mooring design.
  • Complete Rhinoceros → Maxsurf → ANSYS pipeline validated for offshore concept design.
