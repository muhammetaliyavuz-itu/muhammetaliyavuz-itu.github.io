DARPA SUBOFF
Appendage Resistance Prediction

AFF1 · AFF3 · AFF8 configurations · ITTC uncertainty assessment

Project Overview

The DARPA SUBOFF submarine geometry was simulated in STAR-CCM+ to investigate appendage resistance contributions across three configurations: bare hull (AFF1), hull+rudder (AFF3), and hull+sail+rudder (AFF8).

Simulations spanned speeds from 2 to 9.15 m/s. Mesh refinement studies and uncertainty analysis followed ITTC 2017 guidelines. Resistance components (pressure and viscous) were decomposed part-by-part.

Key Findings

  • Minimum deviation of 0.5% from experimental data in optimal mesh configuration.
  • Sail (AFF1→AFF8) contributes significantly more resistance than rudder alone (AFF1→AFF3).
  • Viscous resistance dominates at low speeds; pressure resistance increases with speed.
  • Velocity fields, pressure contours, streamlines, and y⁺ distributions analyzed for all configurations.
  • ITTC mesh refinement ratio and uncertainty quantification methodology strictly applied.
