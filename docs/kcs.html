KCS Hull with Rudder
Resistance Prediction

Free surface CFD · Unsteady RANS · VOF method · 5 simulation cases

Project Overview

The KRISO Container Ship (KCS) hull form with rudder was simulated in STAR-CCM+ to predict calm water resistance. The Volume of Fluid (VOF) method captured free surface effects, with systematic mesh refinement and time step studies to quantify numerical uncertainty following ITTC guidelines.

Five cases were run across three mesh densities and two time steps to identify the optimal configuration.

Key Findings

  • Best accuracy: 0.752% deviation with k-ε model, ~2M cells, Δt = 0.04 s.
  • Free surface wave pattern captured accurately — Kelvin wake clearly visible in VOF solution.
  • Finer mesh (Case 3) with smaller time step increased error, demonstrating time step sensitivity.
  • k-ω SST produced comparable results to k-ε at the same mesh density.
  • Pressure and shear drag components decomposed to isolate hull vs. rudder contributions.
